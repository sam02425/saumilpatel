## Datasets

For this study, we utilized several datasets from **Roboflow Universe** to train and evaluate our object detection models. These datasets provided a diverse and robust training foundation, allowing our models to generalize well across different product types and retail environments.

1. **Grocery Dataset**  
   - **Link**: [Grocery Dataset](https://universe.roboflow.com/lamar-university-venef/grocery-rfn8l)  
   - This dataset contains images of various grocery items, offering a wide range of product types and packaging.

2. **Liquor Dataset**  
   - **Link**: [Liquor Dataset](https://universe.roboflow.com/lamar-university-venef/liquor-data)  
   - Focused on liquor bottles, presenting challenges with similar shapes but varying labels and sizes.

3. **Retail Products Dataset**  
   - **Link**: [Retail Products Dataset](https://universe.roboflow.com/lamar-university-venef/retail-products-vem9o)  
   - A comprehensive dataset with various retail products, allowing the models to learn from multiple item categories and packaging designs.

4. **Key Detection Dataset**  
   - **Link**: [Key Detection Dataset](https://universe.roboflow.com/lamar-university-venef/key-detection-v33ru)  
   - Trained models to detect key points on products, crucial for precise localization and orientation determination.

These datasets were carefully selected to provide a solid foundation for training robust object detection models capable of handling diverse retail product recognition challenges.
